"I make things because I have to. Oil that I push around until it drips right. Wax I melt and burn and scrape back. Clay I throw with my hands until it holds its shape. Prints where the press decides what stays. Digital work that glitches into something more honest than I planned. It's all the same thing really — just me trying to make something real out of the chaos."

— Sundara Heart

How It Gets Made

Encaustic Wax

Heated beeswax fused with pigment. Layered, burned, scraped until it breathes.

Oil Paint

Thick, slow-drying. Dripping like desert sunsets. Emotion trapped in pigment.

Clay

Hand-thrown vessels. Objects you can grip. Totems for ritual and daily life.

Monotype Print

One-of-a-kind impressions. The press decides what survives.

Digital Glitch

Corrupted imagery that feels more honest than the original. Signal and noise.

Music

I write my own stuff. Sometimes record instruments and my own voice. Then collaborate with AI to take it somewhere unexpected.

I'm a multidisciplinary artist and producer based in Santa Fe, New Mexico, with UK roots. This is Silly Goose Recordings — no house style, no rulebook, a lot of ideas.

Honestly? I make whatever wants to come out. Lo-fi, ambient, glitchy, experimental, unhinged, tender — sometimes all of it in one track. I've never stayed in one lane and I'm not about to start. Ask me what genre and I'll say yes. Genre is a filing system. This isn't that. New tracks drop whenever they're ready.

I work in encaustic wax, oil paint, clay, monotype print, and digital — whatever the idea needs. Each one is a different way of doing the same thing: making something out of nothing, with my hands, until it feels true.

I also work as an INELDA-trained death doula through my separate practice, Journeying On — guiding people and families through end-of-life with the same presence I bring to everything else.

Commissions, collabs, and inquiries always welcome.
